# Stay Safe Online - Prevention Checklist
### For College Students in India

---

## General Cybersecurity

1. **Use strong, unique passwords** for every account. Mix uppercase, lowercase, numbers and special characters. Don't use your own name, birthday or something obvious like "password123".

2. **Turn on Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)** on all your important accounts like email, social media and banking apps. Even if someone steals your password, they still can't get in without the second step.

3. **Never share your OTP with anyone.** It doesn't matter if they say they're from your bank, NPCI or even a government office. No real organization ever calls you and asks for your OTP.

4. **Don't click random links** you get on SMS, WhatsApp or email from people you don't know. These can take you to fake websites that steal your login details.

5. **Keep your apps and phone updated.** Updates usually fix security problems. If you're using an old version of an app, it might have gaps that hackers already know about.

6. **Only download apps from official stores** like Google Play Store or Apple App Store. Don't install random APK files from websites, even if someone sends you the link directly.

---

## Financial and UPI Safety

7. **Scanning a QR code means you are SENDING money, not receiving it.** If anyone tells you to scan a QR code to "get a refund" or "receive a payment", that's a scam. You will be the one losing money.

8. **You never need to enter your UPI PIN to receive money.** PIN is only for when you're paying someone. If any app or person is asking for your PIN to send you money, something is very wrong.

---

## Social Media and Identity Safety

9. **Check your privacy settings** on Instagram, Facebook, Snapchat etc. Make sure your phone number, home address and daily routine are not visible to everyone publicly.

10. **Don't respond to random calls or messages about job offers, lottery wins or wrong transfers.** These are classic tricks to get you to either share info or send money quickly before you can think.

---

## If Something Goes Wrong

If you ever become a victim of any cybercrime like UPI fraud, phishing or harassment online, report it here:

- **National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal:** [cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in)
- **Helpline Number:** **1930** (available 24/7)
- You can also go to your nearest police station and file a complaint under the IT Act, 2000.
