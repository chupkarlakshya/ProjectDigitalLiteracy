# Case Study: UPI / Online Payment Fraud

## Type of Cybercrime
**UPI (Unified Payments Interface) / Online Payment Fraud**

---

## What is it?

UPI fraud is basically when someone tricks you into sending them money or giving away your payment details like your UPI PIN, OTP, or bank info. They usually do this through fake calls, fake screenshots, or by pretending to be someone trustworthy.

---

## How It Typically Happens (Step-by-Step)

**Scenario: The "Wrong Transfer" Scam (Realistic Fictional Case)**

1. **Initial Contact:** Riya, a 19-year-old college student in Bhopal, gets a call from some random number. The guy on the call says he accidentally sent her Rs. 5,000 on UPI and wants her to return it.

2. **Building Trust:** He sends her a fake screenshot on WhatsApp showing a "successful transfer". Riya checks her account and doesn't see any money, but the caller keeps insisting it'll show up soon.

3. **The Trap:** He then asks her to scan a QR code he sent to "return" his money. He says something like "just scan this and enter your PIN, it'll send me back my Rs. 5,000."

4. **The Fraud:** Riya scans the QR code and enters her PIN. But instead of sending back Rs. 5,000, Rs. 8,000 gets cut from her account. The QR code was actually a payment request, not a way to receive money. By the time she realizes, the guy has already disconnected.

5. **Consequence:** Riya loses Rs. 8,000 and has no way to get it back because the fraudster was using someone else's bank account (called a mule account) so there's almost no way to trace him.

---

## Who is Usually Targeted?

- Students and young people using UPI for the first time
- People who don't know that scanning a QR code actually sends money, it doesn't receive it
- Anyone who gets calls about fake job offers, lottery prizes, or "wrong transfer" stories

---

## Consequences

- Financial loss which is usually impossible to recover
- A lot of stress and embarrassment afterwards
- Even if you report it, tracing mule accounts takes a long time and recovery is not guaranteed most of the time

---

*This is a fictional scenario written for educational purposes, but it is based on real fraud patterns that happen in India.*
