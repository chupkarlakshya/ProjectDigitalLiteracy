## Reflection

For this task, I used **Canva**, which is a free browser-based graphic design tool with a large library of templates, icons, and fonts. I chose it because it requires no prior design experience and is widely used by students for creating visual content quickly.

My infographic covers three key themes: what digital literacy means, how to stay safe online, and how to build a professional online presence. I used a clean blue-and-white colour scheme with representative icons to make the content visually accessible and easy to read at a glance.

One thing I found **interesting** while making this was how much goes into designing a single page. Every font size, colour choice, and icon placement affects how readable and credible the design looks. I initially packed too much information into the layout and had to significantly simplify my language to keep it clean.

One thing I found **difficult** was deciding what to leave out. Digital literacy is a broad topic, and narrowing it down to three focused sections while still making each meaningful required careful thought. This itself felt like a practical lesson in digital communication — being clear and concise is a skill, not an afterthought.
