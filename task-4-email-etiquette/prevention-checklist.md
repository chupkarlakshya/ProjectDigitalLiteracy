# Stay Safe Online – Prevention Checklist
### For College Students in India

---

##  General Cybersecurity

1. **Use strong, unique passwords** for every account. Use a mix of uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and special characters. Avoid using your name, birthdate, or "password123".

2. **Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)** on all important accounts — email, social media, banking apps. This adds a second layer of security even if your password is stolen.

3. **Never share your OTP with anyone** — not even someone claiming to be from your bank, NPCI, or a government office. Legitimate organisations never ask for OTPs over the phone.

4. **Be cautious of unknown links** — Do not click links received via SMS, WhatsApp, or email from unknown senders. These may lead to phishing websites designed to steal your credentials.

5. **Keep your apps and devices updated** — Security patches are released regularly. Outdated software has known vulnerabilities that attackers exploit.

6. **Use official apps only** — Download banking, UPI, and payment apps only from the Google Play Store or Apple App Store. Avoid APK files from unknown websites.

---

##  Financial & UPI Safety (Mandatory)

7. **Remember: Scanning a QR code sends money — it does not receive money.** If someone asks you to "scan a QR code to receive a refund or payment," it is a scam. You will be sending money, not receiving it.

8. **Never enter your UPI PIN to receive money.** Your PIN is only needed when you are making a payment. No legitimate platform will ask for your PIN to credit funds to your account.

---

##  Social Media & Identity Safety

9. **Review your privacy settings** on all social media platforms. Ensure your phone number, address, and personal details are not publicly visible.

10. **Do not respond to unsolicited job offers, lottery wins, or "wrong transfer" calls.** These are common social engineering tactics used to extract money or personal information.

---

##  Reporting Cybercrime in India

If you are a victim of any cybercrime, including UPI fraud, phishing, or online harassment:

- **National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal:** [cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in)
- **National Cyber Crime Helpline:** **1930** (available 24/7)
- You can also report to your nearest police station or file a complaint under the IT Act, 2000.
